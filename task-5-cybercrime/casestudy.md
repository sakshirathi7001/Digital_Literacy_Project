CASE STUDY : UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD

UPI/online payment fraud is a cybercrime in which criminals trick individuals into authorizing digital payments or revealing sensitive banking details. It commonly exploits the speed and convenience of real-time payment systems.

A typical fraud begins when the scammer contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or messaging apps, often posing as a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. First, the fraudster builds trust by using convincing language or urgent scenarios (e.g., “your account will be blocked”). Next, they send a fake payment request or a malicious link. The victim is instructed to “approve” a request to receive money or to enter their UPI PIN on a phishing page. Once the victim shares their PIN or approves the request, money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account. In some cases, screen-sharing apps are used to observe and capture sensitive details.

Targets are usually everyday smartphone users, small business owners, elderly individuals, or people unfamiliar with digital payment systems. Those expecting payments like sellers are particularly the main target.

The consequences can be severe: Victims may lose their savings They may face emotional distress, and struggle to recover there money Additionally, such fraud undermines trust in digital financial systems.

Preventive awareness: Never share your UPI PINs Verifying payment requests, is crucial to reduce these incidents.
