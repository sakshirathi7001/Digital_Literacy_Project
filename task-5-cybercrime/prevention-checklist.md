‘Stay Safe Online’ Checklist for College Students (India) Use strong, unique passwords for each account on email, banking, social media etc. consider a password manager instead of reusing passwords. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on important apps like email, banking, and social media for an extra layer of security. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking Use mobile data or a trusted network for sensitive activities. Be cautious with links and attachments in emails, WhatsApp, or SMS—especially if they create urgency. Limit what you share on social media, such as phone numbers, address, college ID, or travel plans, as scammers can use this information. Download apps only from official stores (Google Play Store/Apple App Store) and check reviews before installing.

UPI & Financial Safety Tips:

Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank. Always remember you do NOT need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Verify payment requests carefully—check the sender’s name/UPI ID before approving any transaction. Avoid screen-sharing apps with strangers, as they can capture your financial details in real time. Regularly monitor your bank statements and app notifications to spot unauthorized transactions quickly. Log out from shared devices library or lab computers after use.

If something goes wrong:

Immediately report at the National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (quick reporting increases chances of recovering funds)
